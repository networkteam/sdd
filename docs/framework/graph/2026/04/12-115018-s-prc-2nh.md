Pre-flight validator over-corrects on framing and wording when the human has already confirmed the closure through dialogue. Observed in this session: closing s-prc-iwx took three attempts. Attempt 1 rejection was useful (missing commit reference — structural gap). Attempts 2-3 rejected on increasingly pedantic grounds: misinterpretation of d-cpt-vt1 (disagreed with how the contract was characterized), vague evidence (commit hash was present but validator wanted more), pattern unresolved (demanded the general pattern be addressed, not just deferred to s-prc-80l). The final entry is wordier but not meaningfully better than attempt 2. Meanwhile, the d-cpt-axa rejection was genuinely useful — caught an untested topic type that drove real dialogue. The difference: structural gaps (missing evidence, missing test coverage) are high-value catches. Wording/framing nitpicks on entries where human dialogue already produced the judgment are low-value friction. The validator lacks dialogue context — it sees only the entry text and graph, not the conversation that produced the decision to close. Improvement direction: the pre-flight prompt should distinguish structural completeness (missing refs, uncovered requirements, no evidence) from argumentative re-evaluation of the closure rationale. The latter is the human's job via dialogue-before-capture.
