SDD exists in an emerging landscape of AI memory and continuity tools (MemPalace, Zep, Mem0, Letta) that all address the same root problem: context lost when sessions end. These tools optimize for retrieval — making past information findable. SDD optimizes for reasoning — making decisions structured and traceable. But as SDD incorporates retrieval-adjacent capabilities (mining sessions for signals, catch-up summaries, graph search), the boundary blurs. The distinction may not be 'we don't do retrieval' but 'retrieval serves the reasoning loop, not the other way around.' This positioning needs articulation — both for clarity of design decisions and for explaining what SDD is to newcomers.
