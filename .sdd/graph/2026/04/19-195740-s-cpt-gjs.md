Proposes a sixth candidate SDD aspiration — ambient capture: SDD emerges from the work itself, not as a separate mode. Participants stay in their natural activity (chatting, coding, designing, running a meeting) and the graph grows from that motion. Distinct from #3 (overcome ceremony / artifacts): #3 is artifact-facing (no wikis / tickets / docs as separate systems), #6 is activity-facing (no dedicated SDD sessions, no "stop doing work, then do graph work" mode-switch). Together #3 and #6 are the two halves of "minimal overhead" — #3 catches artifact overhead, #6 catches activity overhead. Also distinct from #4 (dialogue shapes decisions): #4 specifies *what* the process is; #6 adds *where* it happens (embedded in existing work, not dedicated sessions).

See [ambient-capture](./19-195740-s-cpt-gjs/ambient-capture.md) for the orthogonality tests vs #3 and #4, Kōgen evidence (narration while cleaning, morning bar, phone after closing), aspiration test, and constellation position.
