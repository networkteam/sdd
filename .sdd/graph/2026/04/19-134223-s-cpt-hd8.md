Proposes a fifth SDD aspiration — autonomy: human and AI participants make better autonomous tactical decisions because the graph holds direction, alignment, and contracts. Extends the four-aspiration set in s-cpt-wiv by naming the motivating value (why the graph work matters) and surfaces a load-bearing layer relationship: higher-layer entries (direction, aspirations, contracts) enable better lower-layer autonomous decisions (tactical, operational). Autonomy is for humans AND agents — agents are autonomous participants in their own domains, not tools within human autonomy.

See [autonomy](./19-134223-s-cpt-hd8/autonomy.md) for why it's a separate aspiration from #1 (coherence vs independence), the layer-relationship insight with Kōgen evidence, the agents-as-participants implication, aspiration test, and constellation position.
