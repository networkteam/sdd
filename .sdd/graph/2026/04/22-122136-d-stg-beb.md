Dialogue shapes decisions. Decisions are shaped by multi-party dialogue — between humans, between humans and agents, between agents — not produced by retrieval alone or by solo generation. Autonomous local decisions draw on context that prior dialogue established.

What this aspiration pushes against:
- **Fragmentation** — reasoning scattered across artifacts that never converge into a coherent record
- **Bypassed dialogue** — decisions produced without multi-party engagement: one participant deciding alone where coordination was needed; tools generating conclusions without humans or agents actually working through the alternatives; retrieval standing in for thinking with context

Retrieval, automation, and tooling all serve the dialogue; they don't replace it. Operational test for any new retrieval or automation capability: *how does it enable better dialogue?* For example — explore alternatives via retrieval, then compress the conclusion into a decision or insight signal.

Closes `s-stg-gtu`, the strategic gap that called for articulating how retrieval-adjacent capabilities (mining, summaries, search) serve the reasoning loop rather than operating as the loop themselves. "Reasoning-first" is a consequence of dialogue shaping decisions: reasoning happens *in* dialogue.
