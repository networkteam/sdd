Instructional text (system prompts, skill playbooks, CLAUDE.md) is an unreliable control mechanism for AI agent behavior. Agents read, understand, and agree with guidelines but fail to follow them consistently when executing tasks. Observed: three evaluation failures in one session despite explicit playbook instructions. This is not specific to the /sdd skill — it applies to any agent-facing workflow. Framework design must assume instructions alone won't produce reliable behavior and incorporate structural enforcement mechanisms (tooling constraints, hooks, gates that block progression) alongside instructional text.
