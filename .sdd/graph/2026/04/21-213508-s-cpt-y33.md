Raises open questions about extending SDD beyond English, with German as the concrete next case. Four investigation strands: (1) whether type/kind/layer labels need localized vocabularies mapped to a stable underlying model, or whether the English tokens stay canonical with localized rendering; (2) whether the `/sdd` skill and pre-flight/summarization templates operate correctly when the dialogue language is German (prompt robustness, template examples, LLM behavior); (3) whether graph-level language belongs in `.sdd/config.yaml` so that an empty conversation running `/sdd` has context for catch-up narration — otherwise the agent has no signal for which language to use; (4) how mixed-language graphs should work when participants use different languages on the same entry chain. Extends the two-type vocabulary contract (20260420-150405-d-cpt-ydf) by testing its portability and relates to the non-technical access gap (20260410-110411-s-tac-7kh) by naming language as another adoption axis, parallel to the agent-neutrality directive (20260413-160358-d-stg-574).
