SDD extends the type system with dedicated `actor` signal and `role` decision kinds, treating participant identity and participation as first-class graph entities rather than free-form references or config state. The approach unifies three currently-separate concerns — canonical-name/alias normalization (`s-cpt-6a6`), participant profiles and routing (`s-cpt-5jk`), and the validator groundwork from `s-tac-7ab` — by giving each a structural slot the graph can query directly and evolve through the normal supersede/close primitives.

Dedicated kinds chosen over (a) fitting participants into existing kinds (`fact` signal + `contract` decision) — rejected on two grounds: free-form semantics would rely on skill and authorial discipline to stay coherent, which collaboration cannot afford; and contract and role are semantically orthogonal — contracts commit to universal invariants (what must always hold across the graph), roles constrain actor-scoped participation (who does what), so conflating them loses both axes. And (b) config-only aliases and roles — rejected because it keeps participant state outside the graph, conflicting with the graph-as-sole-record aspiration (`d-stg-3k0`). First-class treatment also yields immutable history of actor and role evolution as a free byproduct.

This directive commits direction only; sub-decisions follow for actor-kind shape, role-kind shape, frontmatter representation, and bootstrap/retroactive capture. The eventual implementation plan will require superseding the type-system contract (`d-cpt-ydf`) to expand the kind sets from 5+5 to 6+6. Operationalizes the actors/roles exploration named in the focus directive (`d-stg-u2i`).
