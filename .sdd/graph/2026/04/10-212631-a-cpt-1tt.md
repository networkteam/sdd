Researched process reliability for AI agents across three angles: structural enforcement mechanisms in Claude Code (hooks, skills, MCP), the instruction-following gap in agentic coding tools (causes and mitigations), and process enforcement patterns for human-AI collaboration. Key convergence: external enforcement dramatically outperforms instructional guidance. Four control layers (prompt, tool, workflow, validation) — SDD currently relies almost entirely on prompt layer. The generation-validation separation is the highest-leverage pattern. See attachment for full synthesis including framework comparisons, practical patterns, and implications for SDD.
