Raises open questions about scaling SDD to teams where participants hold different roles, skills, and scopes of action. SDD currently treats participants as flat name strings. Four strands: (1) whether a participant profile (role, domain skills, current focus) should filter entry relevance for catch-up and routing; (2) how profiles are captured without becoming management overhead — preferred direction is inferring profiles from graph authorship and periodically checking whether the inferred profile still matches, rather than continuous inference on every interaction; (3) how profiles evolve as people shift focus over time, with the graph itself as the validity signal; (4) whether "who should weigh in on this decision" becomes a first-class concept, where the agent uses authorship history to suggest involvement candidates ("Boris worked on the ordering platform — involve him before this decision"). This is the guidance pattern demonstrated in `docs/story.md`: domain contracts emerged from working patterns and the system routed questions to the person with knowledge. Relates to the participant identity plan (20260417-091917-d-tac-q5p), which establishes name canonicalization but not role, and to the dialogue-shapes-decisions aspiration (20260419-121939-s-cpt-wiv).
