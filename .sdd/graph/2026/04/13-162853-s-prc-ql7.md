The d-prc-zch contract has a blind spot for knowledge-capture actions, where the attachments themselves are the work being made durable rather than a prior commit. The contract requires actions to be captured after the work they describe is committed to Git — which works for actions that record code or system changes (the prior commit grounds the action in durable state) but breaks down for actions whose entire purpose is to formalize knowledge into the graph: research findings, dialogue synthesis, design documents drafted during the capture session. For these actions, the natural workflow is to draft artifacts in scratch space (tmp/, /tmp), then run sdd new --attach to copy them into the graph's permanent attachment directory, then commit entry plus attachments together. The "prior commit" the contract requires does not exist because the artifacts arrive durable as part of the entry creation, not before. Observed during the capture of a-cpt-xbv (research on SDD distribution tooling). Pre-flight rejected three attempts to capture the action: first without any acknowledgment of the constraint, second with the dialogue context folded into the entry text, third explicitly noting the user's confirmation in dialogue. Pre-flight rejected each time, with the third rejection being explicit and correct in its reasoning: "active contracts require formal override, not dialogue acknowledgment." The action was eventually captured with --skip-preflight after dialogue with the user. Two ways to address: (a) refine d-prc-zch to distinguish work-recording actions (which require prior commit) from knowledge-capture actions (which produce durable artifacts via --attach); the refinement could specify that --attach is the contract's "commit" for the artifacts it brings in. (b) Supersede d-prc-zch with a more nuanced contract that handles both action classes. Either way, the pre-flight validator's behavior is sound — contracts cannot be overridden by dialogue, only by formal supersedes. The gap is in the contract's framing, not the validator. Note: this signal itself produced no code changes and is not subject to d-prc-zch (signals are not constrained by it), which is itself a small data point about the contract's scope.
