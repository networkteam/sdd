The SDD graph lacks an explicit focus/priority layer between perpetual aspirations and active plans/WIP — a way to declare "of all open ends, here is what matters now." Without it, accumulation of open signals and decisions makes traversal and alignment harder as the graph grows: participants can't quickly see which threads should get attention, and catch-up treats all open entries as roughly equal in salience.

Why it matters: lots of open ends are harder to tackle, and focus creates alignment across participants — directly grounding the coherence aspiration (d-stg-qlt).

Three candidate shapes were dialogued:

1. **Use existing primitives (preferred starting point)** — three sub-approaches, all using existing vocabulary:
   - **1a. Focus directive** — a strategic-or-conceptual directive refs 2–3 priority entries with rationale, superseded when priorities shift. Rationale-heavy, history-of-attention as a directive chain.
   - **1b. Focus activity** — an activity refs priority entries and dispatches attention on them, closed by a done signal when refs complete. Lower-ceremony, with a natural heartbeat.
   - **1c. Priority frontmatter on activities** — add a `priority` field (e.g. low/medium/high/critical) to activity frontmatter; focus emerges as a query over activity priorities rather than a pointer entry. Distributed rather than centralized; could extend to other entry kinds.

   The 1a/1b choice depends on whether focus shifts mid-stream (supersede rhythm) or runs to completion (done-signal rhythm); 1c is orthogonal and could combine with either. Left as a live sub-decision for when the gap is addressed.

2. **Dedicated priority/focus kind** — sharper semantics but adds to the just-stabilized 10-kind vocabulary (d-cpt-ydf); reach for it only if the 1a–1c patterns prove insufficient.

3. **Role-scoped priorities** — layered on top of 1 or 2, filters at read time by the participant's current role. Depends on first-class actors/roles (s-cpt-6ct).

Implications beyond the graph model:

- **Catch-up rendering**: at scale, catch-up should lead with entries matching the current focus, then group or summarize the rest into a collapsed parked/background section, with drill-down on explicit request. Reframes catch-up from exhaustive enumeration to focus-primary briefing.
- **Skill guidance**: the `/sdd` skill must know the focus pattern and proactively suggest applying it when open-ends accumulate past a threshold ("lots of entries, no clear focus — want to capture a focus directive?"). Without this prompt, the mechanism stays dormant and catch-up degrades silently as the graph scales.
- **Narrative illustration**: `docs/story.md` shows catch-up prioritizing within small scope (Scene 2) and role-routing via contracts (Scene 4), but has no scene demonstrating explicit focus capture at the scale that motivates this gap — likely a story extension once the mechanism lands.
