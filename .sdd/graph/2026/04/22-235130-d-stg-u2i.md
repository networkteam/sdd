The current strategic focus is adopting SDD into 1–2 real projects to validate the framework under actual use, with three supporting explorations running in parallel: German-language portability (`s-cpt-y33`) because one target project is German, first-class actors/roles (`s-cpt-6ct`) because multi-participant graphs require role-based routing and filtering, and background sync (`d-tac-n6y`) which is load-bearing for multi-participant collaboration and the natural next tactical step. This focus grounds the alignment aspiration (`d-stg-qlt`) — adoption is where alignment gets tested in practice — and directly addresses the adoption gap (`s-stg-qg0`) by picking a concrete path forward. Supersede with a new focus directive when priorities shift or adoption reaches a milestone that unlocks a new phase.
