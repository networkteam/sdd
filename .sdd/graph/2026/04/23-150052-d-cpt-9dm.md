The `role` decision kind commits "actor X participates as Y" — scoping both SDD-internal contribution shape (who reviews, captures, grooms, evaluates) and the broader project-facing profile (domain expertise, skills, areas of focus, what the actor wants to bring). Role is orthogonal to contract per the parent directive (`d-cpt-pc0`): roles constrain actor-scoped participation (who brings what, who does what), contracts commit to universal invariants (what must always hold across the graph).

Frontmatter: `actor: <canonical-name>` required — references the actor's canonical name rather than a specific entry ID, so the role stays stable across actor supersessions. Refs must include the current actor entry ID at creation time, mechanically validated by the CLI (`sdd new` and `sdd lint`) against active — non-superseded, non-closed — actor signals whose canonical matches. The canonical-name-over-entry-id split keeps identity stable while preserving graph density and cross-reference integrity.

Scope and content: prose in the description, covering what the actor offers (skills, domain expertise, interests) and what they do (SDD-internal duties, project-level engagement, thread focus). Role captures specialization and preferred contribution shape rather than raw capability — e.g. Ben is the DB-heavy backend specialist and Jana the frontend guru for micro-animations, even though both can technically do fullstack. No structured action taxonomy (reviewer / capturer / groomer) or skill vocabulary initially. Keep minimum surface; let real usage reveal which axes are worth structuring later.

Lifecycle: a role decision is superseded when the participation pattern shifts — scope expands or narrows, skills or interests evolve, responsibilities change. It is closed when the role ends (actor no longer holds it) via a directive carrying retirement rationale per the stable-kind retirement rules. Default confidence medium — role is an evolving commitment, unlike actor which is an observational fact. Multiple roles per actor are permitted: one actor can hold concurrent role decisions covering different threads, domains, or contribution shapes.

Layer: `process` — same as actor, framework-meta about how we work and who brings what. File path follows standard `YYYY/MM/DD-HHmmss-d-prc-xxx.md`.

Refines `d-cpt-pc0` (parent) and `d-cpt-cye` (actor shape); settles the role half. Frontmatter representation elsewhere in the graph, and bootstrap/retroactive capture remain as open sub-decisions.
