Pre-flight rejection of d-cpt-ah1 for missing the d-cpt-vt1 reference revealed an emergent property: pre-flight doesn't just check structural completeness of individual entries — it actively densifies the graph by forcing entries to cite the decisions they build on. As this happens continuously, the network grows tighter: implicit lineage (legible only to the author at the moment of writing) gets converted into explicit refs that future readers and traversal can follow. Builds on s-prc-awd (positive eval that pre-flight shifted toward structural completeness checks) — graph densification is a second-order benefit of that same shift. Implication: pre-flight is doing graph-maintenance work as a side-effect of per-entry validation, not just per-entry quality control.
