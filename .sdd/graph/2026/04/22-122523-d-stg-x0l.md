Non-developer participants engage with SDD directly. Business owners, operators, domain experts, creators — anyone whose work shapes decisions — contribute signals, read the graph, and join dialogue without needing a developer to translate for them. SDD reaches them in surfaces they already use: chat, voice, GUI alongside CLI.

What this aspiration pushes against:
- **Developer mediation** — non-technical participants can only act by asking someone who can operate the tools to act on their behalf
- **Interface monoculture** — SDD accessible only through surfaces the participant doesn't use (e.g., CLI for someone who lives in chat)

Distinct from the alignment aspiration (`20260422-122317-d-stg-qlt`) — alignment could happen developer-only (a programmer and their agent staying coherent); reaching Mara the business owner and Jun the roaster (Kōgen) without developer mediation is a separate pull, a product choice about who the graph is for.

Branches from the dialogue aspiration (`20260422-122136-d-stg-beb`): non-technical access *requires* dialogue as the interface. CLI and static documents are developer-shaped; chat/voice/GUI fit dialogue natively.

Evidence in `s-cpt-wiv`'s attachment: `s-stg-qg0` (adoption friction), `s-tac-7kh` (CLI barrier for non-technical users), `s-tac-bta` (sdd mirror → Obsidian as GUI path).
