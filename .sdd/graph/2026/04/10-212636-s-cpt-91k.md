Research confirms that the generation-validation separation is the highest-leverage reliability pattern for agent systems. The agent that acts cannot reliably certify its own compliance — this is structural (training optimizes for plausible continuation, not durable policy execution; self-assessment is biased; errors compound). For SDD, this means agent unreliability is a permanent design constraint, not a gap to close with better instructions. Every critical process step needs external verification: the graph enforces process (types, refs, contracts), external validators enforce compliance (pre-flight in sdd new), humans enforce authority (dialogue, confirmation, override). Self-monitoring is advisory input, never the sole gate. The pre-flight validator (d-tac-s6w) is the first instance of this principle — it should extend to all critical boundaries.
