The `actor` signal kind takes a minimal structural frontmatter — `canonical: <name>` required as the stable handle across supersessions, and optional `aliases: [<name>, ...]` listing alternative name forms seen in external sources. External context (job title, organization, focus areas, authoring locale) lives in the prose description rather than frontmatter, because it changes too often to warrant supersede ceremony and is not queryable in a structural way.

Lifecycle: an actor signal is superseded when any structurally-indexed identity fact shifts (canonical name change, alias added or removed, spelling correction). It is closed when the actor is no longer relevant to the graph (left the project, will not participate further), via a directive carrying retirement rationale per the stable-kind retirement rules. Default confidence is high — actor signals are observational facts of existence.

Layer: `process`. Actor records are framework-meta facts about how we work and who works, and the process layer keeps file-path and rendering conventions intact. Path shape follows the standard `YYYY/MM/DD-HHmmss-s-prc-xxx.md`. Description narrative is prose covering who the actor is in external terms a reader needs for refs elsewhere, with optional notes on first appearance or project context.

Refines the parent directive (`d-cpt-pc0`) that committed to first-class actor/role kinds; this decision fixes the actor half of the shape. Role-kind shape, frontmatter representation elsewhere in the graph, and bootstrap/retroactive capture remain as open sub-decisions.
