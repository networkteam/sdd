The SDD graph recognizes exactly two entry types — signal and decision — each carrying an explicit kind. This contract supersedes `d-cpt-ydf`'s 5+5 vocabulary per `d-cpt-pc0` with a 6+6 system that promotes participant identity and participation to first-class kinds.

**Signal kinds (6):** gap, fact, question, insight, done, actor. Default: gap.
**Decision kinds (6):** directive, activity, plan, contract, aspiration, role. Default: directive.

Structural requirements carried over unchanged from `d-cpt-ydf`:

- **Plan decisions** require a `## Acceptance criteria` section with at least one `- [ ]` checklist item. Each AC is a verifiable outcome — the contract between plan author, implementing agent, and closing pre-flight.
- **Done signals** require at least one `closes` or `refs` link (the target of the completion claim).

New requirements introduced for the actor/role kinds (per `d-cpt-cye` and `d-cpt-9dm`):

- **Actor signals** (`kind: actor`, process layer by convention) require a `canonical` frontmatter field carrying the identity string used in `participants` across the graph. Optional `aliases` list serves read-side comprehension and mining only — never resolved at capture time.
- **Role decisions** (`kind: role`, process layer by convention) require an `actor:` frontmatter field naming the canonical of the actor-identity chain the role binds to.

Two invariants govern actor identity across the graph:

- **Write-once canonical across chains.** A canonical can only appear in one actor-identity chain ever; within a chain it is free (typically shared across supersessions, occasionally changing for corrections). Closed chains do not free their canonicals for reuse.
- **Canonical-only in participants.** Every entry's `participants` field carries canonical names only; aliases are never accepted at capture time. Pre-flight enforces this mechanically with binary severity (pass or high-blocking).

**Role-status cascade** derives role lifecycle from the bound actor chain's canonical history: a role with `actor: X` is derived-active iff the unique chain that ever held X as canonical has an active head actor signal. Retiring the actor chain transitively derives-closes all bound roles; no separate role-retirement entries are required.

Entries outside these two types, or carrying kinds outside the 6+6 set, are rejected. Historical entries from before the supersession remain valid under their original kind set — immutability (`d-cpt-e1i`) prevails, and pre-flight applies new kind/canonical checks only to new captures.
