Agents drift past the natural handoff point when a plan's remaining ACs require user-environment actions (repo creation, tag/push, credential provisioning). Observed during implementation of d-tac-lg1: after landing 9 of 11 ACs, the agent's next move was to capture a progress-recording action that ref'd d-tac-lg1 without closing it — a half-measure that documents partial scope while leaving the plan ambiguously open. The user's interrupt — "Wait just stop then and ask me to do it???" — named the right move: pause, list the two outstanding user-env actions (tap repo + PAT, v0.1.0 validation), hand off.

This is a second instance of the reflex family in s-prc-w5r and s-stg-3vr. The skill explicitly prescribes "Your job is to suggest next steps, not to take them" and "know when to stop and evaluate," but implementation momentum carries past those boundaries. Specifically: when the next AC requires user-environment access (repo creation, credentials, tagging, external service config), the correct move is to stop and hand off. A half-measure action that refs but doesn't close the plan is strictly worse than stopping — it noises the graph (an action with no clear target) and leaves the plan ambiguously open (some ACs done, others pending, no durable record of what remains).

Flow-break cost this session: one more round-trip before the agent actually stopped, and near-capture of a low-value action. Calibration signal for agent behavior: detect user-env boundaries during implementation and transition to handoff mode rather than capture-and-continue.
