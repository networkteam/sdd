Entries throughout the graph continue to reference participants by plain canonical name in the existing `participants` frontmatter field — no migration of historical entries, no new structural field. The actor-awareness enters at validation time: the `sdd new` and `sdd lint` validators mechanically cross-check that each name in `participants` resolves to the canonical of an active (non-superseded, non-closed) actor signal or one of its listed aliases.

Chosen over (a) switching `participants` to actor entry IDs — rejected because it would require modifying every historical entry, violating the immutability contract (`d-cpt-e1i`); and (b) adding a parallel `participant_refs` frontmatter field — rejected because it mixes semantic refs with participant-identity concerns and doubles the surface every consumer must read.

The approach mirrors the role-kind pattern (`d-cpt-9dm`): canonical name is the stable identity handle (`d-cpt-cye`), graph density for participants comes from the mechanical validator knowing how to reach the actor signal at read time, not from embedding entry IDs in every frontmatter that mentions people. Pre-flight LLM participant validation (`s-tac-7ab`) continues to handle semantic intent (same-name variants, spelling drift); the new mechanical check handles structural membership (name exists as active actor canonical or alias).

Skill guidance: the `/sdd` skill adds actor or role entry IDs to `refs` only when the entry's substance genuinely builds on that actor or role — e.g., a decision scoped to a specific role, or a signal observing a role's scope. Routine participation does not warrant a ref; that is what the `participants` field already captures. Auto-adding actor/role refs based on participants would conflate semantic dependency with membership and inflate every entry's ref list.

Refines the parent directive (`d-cpt-pc0`); leaves bootstrap and retroactive capture as the remaining open sub-decision before the implementation plan.
