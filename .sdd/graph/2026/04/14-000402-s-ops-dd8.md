`sdd show` produces oversized output for well-connected entries, burning tokens in catch-up / explore / pre-flight. Recursive ref/closes/supersedes expansion has no bound, so well-connected decisions print their full transitive context every time they're shown. This directly inflates `/sdd-catchup`, `/sdd-explore`, and pre-flight validation cost and latency.

Proposed direction (unvalidated): introduce a `--max-depth` on `sdd show` that bounds recursion with a truncation marker. Pair it with BFS traversal so dedup prints each entry at its shallowest occurrence, with later encounters replaced by a cross-reference marker ("see above: {id}"). Open questions: what the default depth should be, whether fan-out (many direct refs on one entry) needs a separate cap, and whether the dedup scope is per-invocation or per-entry.
