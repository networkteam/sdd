Pre-flight currently performs LLM-based presence-of-field checks for entry metadata — the a-prc-dix corpus surfaces real rejections for 'Missing Kind field in header' and 'Missing Confidence field'. These are deterministic structural checks better handled at CLI parse time: required fields can error cleanly without LLM judgment, faster, and don't consume pre-flight prompt budget or risk indeterminacy. Proposes (a) make Confidence required at the CLI level (no default — confidence is the author's deliberate self-assessment and should not be silently defaulted), (b) rely on the existing Kind default-to-directive for decisions (per a-ops-4zc), and (c) remove presence-of-metadata-field checks from LLM templates so pre-flight focuses on judgments the LLM is uniquely good at: cross-reference contradictions, plan-requirement coverage, contract-spirit deviations. Named as out-of-scope in d-prc-g1h; this signal formalizes it for follow-up implementation after the combined plan lands.
