Making participants/actors first-class entries in the graph would unify three currently-separate concerns: (a) canonical identity (d-tac-6z1 solved this for the local participant via config; s-cpt-6a6 asks how to extend beyond), (b) alternative-form aliasing (same question, harder for non-local participants), and (c) participant profiles — roles, domain skills, focus (s-cpt-5jk). The proposed shape maps cleanly to the framework's signal/decision split:

An **actor** signal kind declares "this person exists as a participant, canonical C, aliases [...]" — observational, fact-shaped: supersedable when new information emerges (additional alias, corrected canonical), closable if the record is no longer relevant to the graph. External role context (job title, org affiliation) lives as metadata on the actor signal.

A **role** decision kind commits "this actor participates in the SDD graph as X — reviews decisions on thread Y, captures signals for area Z, grooms stale entries in scope W" — superseded when the participation pattern shifts, closed when the role ends. Role is the actor's function *inside* the SDD process (who reviews what, who grooms where, who evaluates which done signals), not their organizational job title.

Pre-flight participant validation could then resolve proposed names against actor signals directly — no config extension needed for aliases, no heuristic disambiguation for non-local voices. And role decisions carry the semantic s-cpt-5jk wants for routing: "who should weigh in on thread X" becomes a queryable property of the graph.

Open tensions to work through in future dialogue:

1. Existing entries reference participants by plain string name. An actor-aware world could keep strings (flat, readable) with lookup happening in the validator, or introduce entity refs in the frontmatter (structured, queryable — invasive to the file format and migration story).
2. Supersede-vs-close semantics for role decisions need to match how participation actually shifts — role changes, someone leaves and returns, someone contributes only to a specific thread.
3. Bootstrap — actor signals would need to precede other entries referencing them, or capture retroactively without breaking the immutability contract. Ordering / retrospective-capture rules need thought.
4. Adding new kinds (`actor`, `role`) is a framework-level extension beyond the current kind sets. Worth weighing against fitting actors into existing kinds (e.g. `fact` signal + `contract` decision) — dedicated kinds sharpen semantics, but every new kind raises the bar for framework simplicity.

Emerged from smoke-testing d-tac-6z1 and noticing that s-cpt-6a6 and s-cpt-5jk share a root: both want more structure around participants than a single-string name can carry.
