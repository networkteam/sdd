Local-model summary testing reveals that the single shared summary template produces consistent regressions on gemma4:26b: generic "This signal identifies..." openers (ignoring the self-describing-first-sentence rule), lost specificity (dropped concrete examples, collapsed enumerations, missing source anchors like "story.md proposes..."), and occasional self-referencing IDs in the summary text. The pluggable provider layer (d-tac-bes) enables multi-model use but does not make prompts portable — identical prompts produce qualitatively different output across models. Implies either template hardening (explicit "do not reference your own ID" rule, concrete first-sentence exemplars) or model-specific prompt variants.

Observed on s-stg-8d3, s-cpt-ukp, s-prc-dk3, s-prc-omw during a gemma4:26b batch run at concurrency 2 while testing d-tac-bes. Sample small but patterns consistent across the four.
