Implemented the 6+6 kind type-system extension closing plan `d-cpt-d34`. All 18 acceptance criteria addressed; the framework now treats participant identity and participation as first-class graph entries with mechanical canonical validation, cascade-based role status, and native surfacing in `sdd status`.

**AC 1 — 6+6 kind contract.** Captured `d-cpt-ygn` superseding `d-cpt-ydf` with 6 signal kinds (`gap`, `fact`, `question`, `insight`, `done`, `actor`) and 6 decision kinds (`directive`, `activity`, `plan`, `contract`, `aspiration`, `role`). Contract carries over structural requirements (plan AC section, done closes/refs) and adds actor/role-specific shape plus the canonical-only-in-participants and write-once-across-chains invariants.

**AC 2 — Actor signal kind.** `KindActor` added to signal kinds set (`internal/model/entry.go`). Entry struct gains `Canonical` and `Aliases` fields; frontmatter parse/format covers both. `validateActorFrontmatter` requires `canonical`, warns on non-process layer. `sdd new s prc --kind actor` with `--skip-preflight` creates valid entries; writing a canonical on a non-actor entry is silently tolerated (field has no meaning there).

**AC 3 — Role decision kind.** `KindRole` added to decision kinds set. Entry struct gains `Actor` field; frontmatter round-trips. `validateRoleFrontmatter` requires `actor`, warns on non-process layer. `sdd new d prc --kind role` creates valid role entries.

**AC 4 — Pre-flight actor/role rubrics.** New LLM templates `actor_capture.tmpl` and `role_capture.tmpl` focus on prose quality (identity introduction, contribution shape, orthogonality-to-contract). `selectCheckType` routes actor and role kinds to these templates before generic dispatch. Mechanical frontmatter validation runs separately.

**AC 5 — Actor canonical write-once invariant.** `mechanicalPreflight` in `internal/finders/preflight_mechanical.go` detects the chain being extended via `Supersedes` and rejects cross-chain canonical reuse with a `high`-severity `actor-canonical-reused` finding. Within-chain reuse is allowed; closed chains remain "locked" (canonicals don't free for reuse).

**AC 6 — Participant coverage check.** `participantCoverageFindings` enforces that every name in a new entry's `Participants` matches an active actor's canonical (no alias resolution). Severity is binary (pass or high). Self-transitioning grace mode: when the graph has zero active actor signals, the check is skipped entirely; the first actor capture activates enforcement.

**AC 7 — Role mechanical check.** `roleMechanicalFindings` requires the role's `Actor` to match the current head canonical of an active chain AND `Refs` to include that head's entry ID. Two findings categories (`role-canonical-mismatch`, `role-refs-missing-head`), both high-severity and capture-time only.

**AC 8 — Role status cascade.** `internal/model/actor.go` holds `ActorChain`, `ActorChains`, `ChainForCanonical`, `ResolveRoleChain`, `ActiveActorHeads`, `ActiveRoles` — all pure model functions per the push-logic-down rule. `DerivedStatus` dispatches role entries through `deriveRoleStatus` which walks full canonical history: role is `active`, `cascade-closed` (chain head closed), or `cascade-orphan` (canonical resolves to no chain). `Graph.Filter` with `OpenOnly` respects cascade closure.

**AC 9 — LLM participant validator retired.** `participants.tmpl` deleted; eight consumer templates stripped of their `{{ template "participants" . -}}` include. `LocalCanonical` and `EstablishedParticipants` fields removed from `preflightContext`; `localCanonical` argument removed from `llm.Preflight`. The finder's `localParticipant()` helper remains — it still feeds `sdd status` and future uses.

**ACs 10-13 — Lint checks.** `Graph.validate` gains four post-per-entry cross-graph passes: `validateParticipantCoverage` (every entry's participants must match active canonical), `validateAliasAmbiguity` (aliases colliding across active actors, informational), `validateActorInvariant` (same canonical across chains, defense-in-depth), `validateRoleOrphans` (role actor doesn't resolve). Findings attach to the offending entries so `sdd lint` output clusters naturally.

**AC 14 — `sdd list --kind actor|role`.** Filter help text updated for the 6+6 vocabulary in both `sdd new --kind` and `sdd list --kind`. The existing `Graph.Filter` kind-matching logic already routes actor/role entries without further changes.

**AC 15 — `sdd status` Participants block.** `query.ParticipantGroup` couples an active actor head with its derived-active roles. `finders.activeParticipantGroups` materializes the grouping — finder owns the ordering; presenter is pure view layer. `presenters.renderParticipantsSection` renders the block after existing sections, omitting when grace. Entry lines use the shared `EntryLine` helper (same format as other sections, agent-first CLI consistency).

**AC 16 — framework-concepts.md updates.** Signal kinds table expanded to 6 rows; decision kinds table expanded to 6 rows. New "Actors and Roles" section documents: kind shape, canonical-only-in-participants rule, write-once canonical invariant, role-status chain-canonical-history cascade with capture-time vs derivation-time split, orphan handling, Participants-block surfacing.

**AC 17 — main `/sdd` SKILL.md guidance.** New sub-section "Write canonical names, never aliases" covers canonical-only discipline in `--participants`, auto-ref avoidance for routine participation, grace-mode behavior, and the surfacing point (Participants block + `sdd list --kind actor|role`, not existing kind sections).

**AC 18 — Unit tests.** `internal/model/actor_test.go` covers chain traversal (single-entry, typo-correction-within-chain), role status derivation (active, cascade-closed, within-chain correction keeping old role active, orphan), active actor heads (chain-retirement exclusion), active role ordering, and all four lint checks (participant coverage, alias ambiguity, invariant, orphan). `internal/finders/preflight_test.go` covers mechanical participant coverage (match, unknown, grace, alias-does-not-match), actor write-once (new chain, within-chain, cross-chain), role mechanical checks (canonical mismatch, missing head ref, valid). `internal/presenters/status_test.go` covers Participants block rendering (grouping, ordering after main sections, grace suppression). Snapshot for the validation message updated.
