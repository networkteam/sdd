Agent reasoning conflates captured entries (signals, unclosed decisions, plans) with implemented reality. Observed this session: cited s-prc-ljg's phase-aware pre-flight severity behavior as if it were active logic when the signal is only a proposal — the current pre-flight has no phase awareness; the LLM happened to pick medium severity unrelated to that proposal.

Proposed rule for the /sdd base skill: non-closed entries are proposals, not descriptions of current state. An entry becomes authoritative only when a closing action explicitly declares what was done. When reasoning from the graph, distinguish "the graph says X might become true" from "the graph shows X was done."

This strengthens the role of closing actions (they must declare concrete work, not just "fulfilled") and guards against the drift where open signals accrue quiet authority over time.
