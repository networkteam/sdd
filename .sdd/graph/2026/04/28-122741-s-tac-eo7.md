`sdd init --scope project|global` chooses where the skill bundle is installed but does not persist the choice in `.sdd/config.yaml` (or `meta.json`). A subsequent `sdd init` falls back to defaults rather than reading the recorded scope, which can produce accidental global installs when the project already has skills locally, or missed installs when a clone needs them at the previously-chosen scope. Persisting the scope in `.sdd/config.yaml` lets `sdd init` recognize the project's setup intent consistently across contributors and runs, and is a prerequisite for the readiness-check behavior proposed in the companion fresh-clone setup gap (s-tac-z8o).
