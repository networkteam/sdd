Participants — human and AI — act autonomously in their domains, supported by shared higher-layer context the graph holds. Most decisions are made locally by whoever has the domain knowledge, with confidence they'll cohere with the rest because direction, alignment, and contracts are already visible to everyone. Synchronous coordination happens when needed; it isn't the default path.

What this aspiration pushes against:
- **Coordination overhead** — blocking approvals or mandatory synchronous coordination where shared higher-layer context would suffice. A rule like "require human approval before any agent capture" coordinates agreement across participants (which the alignment aspiration `20260422-122317-d-stg-qlt` might endorse) but strips domain-appropriate autonomy (which this aspiration pulls toward) — constellation tension, resolved case by case.
- **Agents-as-tools framing** — treating AI agents as instruments that execute human decisions rather than autonomous participants who capture signals, make tactical decisions in their domains, and flag when scope exceeds their authority

Load-bearing structural insight: higher-layer entries (aspirations, contracts, directives) compress collective agreement into shared context that enables many lower-layer autonomous decisions. Without clear higher-layer content, tactical work stalls for clarification or drifts from lack of shared grounding.

Distinct from the alignment aspiration (`20260422-122317-d-stg-qlt`) — alignment is the coherence pull; autonomy is the independence pull. A decision can serve one while hurting the other; proposals are evaluated against the constellation, not a single star.

Branches from the dialogue aspiration (`20260422-122136-d-stg-beb`): autonomy is what dialogue-shaped decisions produce. The mechanism (dialogue at higher layers) serves the value (autonomous local action in domains).

Evidence in `s-cpt-hd8`'s attachment: Kōgen-story examples where Priya picks Stripe alone, Mara decides shipping pricing alone, Jun decides share-note behavior alone, the implementation agent picks hosted-vs-embedded Stripe alone — each autonomous within higher-layer context already agreed.
