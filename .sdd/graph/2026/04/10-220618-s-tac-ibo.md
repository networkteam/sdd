sdd show multi-entry output is a flat stream with no hierarchy between primary (requested) entries and referenced (context) entries. The catch-up sub-skill asks for ~15 primary entries, each with 3-5 refs, producing ~60 entries at the same level. The consumer must reverse-engineer which entries were the query targets vs. pulled-in context. The '(shown above)' deduplication is a space optimization for human terminal readers, not a relational pointer — it actually makes primary/context distinction harder by removing the content that would help identify an entry's role. A hierarchical output — primaries as top-level items, their refs nested underneath with clear visual hierarchy — would preserve query intent and let the consumer immediately see what to reason about vs. what's background. The refs within entries are sufficient for understanding relationships; the missing piece is which entries are the focus of attention.
