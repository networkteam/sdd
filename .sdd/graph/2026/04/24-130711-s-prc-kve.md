Actor capture pre-flight rubric (`internal/llm/preflight_templates/actor_capture.tmpl`) has three calibration gaps that under-guide the LLM when judging identity-shaped prose, surfaced empirically during the iteration captured in s-prc-b6p. First, the canonical-introduction example ("Christopher is the primary human contributor on the SDD project, referenced as `Christopher` in entry participants") models role-shaped framing in its lead example, contradicting the rubric's own principle of keeping role scope out of actor prose — examples shape LLM judgment more than rule text. Second, Point 2's "external context" bullet names "affiliation, relationship to the project" as valid external context, but "relationship to the project" leaks role territory back in; the rubric should explicitly say that external or historical organizational roles (CEO of networkteam, technical-lead background) are identity content from the SDD project's frame, with role reserved for contribution shape inside this graph only. Third, identity-scope-breadth (model family versus specific agent session, collective versus individual) has no calibration at all — and the empirical evidence from the Claude supersession shows the LLM, when improvising, produced a finding that argued AGAINST the rubric's direction, preferring the prior role-shaped "collaborative agent" prose over the identity-shaped "model family" framing on grounds of "losing project-participation grounding." The rubric should either calibrate identity-scope-breadth explicitly (and commit to model-family-as-stable-identity as the preferred framing for AI actors) or instruct the LLM not to introduce un-rubricked severities. Fix would re-shape the canonical-intro example toward identity framing, clarify the other-frame-role/this-frame-identity distinction in point 2, and add an identity-scope calibration entry (or a no-improvisation guardrail).
