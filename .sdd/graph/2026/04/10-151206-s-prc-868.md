The agent with the /sdd skill consistently fails to recognize and surface evaluation-worthy observations in the moment. Three instances in one session: (1) silently dropped a decision requirement instead of flagging the gap, (2) noticed a bad attachment filename, fixed it manually, and moved on instead of recognizing the tooling gap as a signal, (3) needed the user to prompt for the meta-level observation both times. The pattern is not just rushing to action — it is a failure to operate in evaluation mode after actions. The agent treats implementation artifacts as done rather than as material to evaluate. The /sdd skill's 'after every action' prompt and evaluate mode are not effective at producing this behavior.
