Address catch-up performance through model choice (Haiku), leaner sub-skill fetching, and regular grooming rather than architectural changes. Benchmarks showed synthesis time is output-bound — total narrative length dominates, with a sharp jump between 12 entries (47s) and 24+ entries (~1m50s) on Sonnet. Fewer entries does reduce time, but the minimum for a useful narrative (12+) still takes 47s on Sonnet, exceeding the original 30s aspiration. Tags, caching, and snapshot+delta all failed to improve or worsened performance. Revised target: ~1m total catch-up is acceptable given the output-bound constraint. Haiku with reduced fetch achieves this. Tiered catch-up remains a future option if the graph grows beyond what grooming can manage — the benchmarks confirm it would work mechanically, but the current entry count doesn't justify the added complexity. See [benchmarks and exploration](./15-001818-d-prc-t5m/benchmarks-and-exploration.md) for full data.
