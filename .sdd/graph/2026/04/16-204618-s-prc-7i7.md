Pre-flight findings of the pattern 'plan AC does not address an active contract directly implicated by the plan's scope' recur at medium severity during plan capture, leaving the author to choose between superseding a freshly-captured immutable plan and accepting the gap until closing-time pre-flight catches it. This specific sub-pattern should escalate to high at plan phase, blocking capture until ACs cover the implicated contracts. Scoped narrowly: only contracts the plan directly implicates (e.g. a CSV parser bug fix implicates d-prc-8vh 'bug fix requires regression test'; a pure refactor plan does not); not every active contract. Refines s-prc-ljg (phase-aware severity) with a concrete escalation sub-rule. Observed concretely on d-tac-955 where the CSV parser bug-fix AC did not explicitly commit to a regression test, triggering a medium finding that was legitimate but non-blocking; the plan was then captured as-is with the test commitment implicit via contract.
