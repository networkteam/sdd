Mined the SDD graph and 14 days of Claude Code session history to build an empirical corpus grounding the upcoming combined plan for confidence-scored pre-flight verdicts and acceptance criteria in plans. Triggered by s-prc-316's diagnosis of find-something bias in the binary PASS/FAIL format — calibration anchors needed to be grounded in real rejections rather than intuition. Two parallel sub-agents produced (a) a graph catalog of 28 pre-flight-related entries across six reasoning chains, and (b) a verbatim session corpus of 37 actual pre-flight FAILs from 300 sdd new events. Headline findings: ~35% outright false-positive rate, ~57% including borderlines; 9 of 12 sessions with FAILs resorted to --skip-preflight; closing_decision (50%) and closing_action (38%) dominate FP rates; supersedes had 0% FP. Nine anti-pattern categories named with verbatim evidence (prose-completeness checking, editorial rewriting, confidence policing, ritual formatting demands, CQRS/architecture theater, strategic grounding stifling, structural nitpicks belonging in lint, same-commit forensics, contract over-application). Five high-severity anchor patterns named with cross-reference contradiction as the gold standard. Calibration examples in the combined plan draw verbatim from this corpus where available. See [graph catalog](./15-112708-a-prc-dix/preflight-graph-catalog.md) and [session corpus](./15-112708-a-prc-dix/preflight-session-corpus.md) for detail.
