Explore mode is 'handle an open entry' — take something from the graph that needs attention and work through it in dialogue until the next graph move is clear. That move might be a decision, an action definition, sub-decision decomposition, closing as stale, or transitioning to implementation — but the mode doesn't prescribe which. It assembles context (forked: full chain, downstream refs, status), frames the entry ('what does this need?'), and drives dialogue until something crystallizes. The outcome is always a graph change. The skill should carry playbook moves for common scenarios (open signal without decisions, decision too big to act on, decision needing an action, tensions between entries, stale entries) — not as a flowchart but as pattern recognition that helps guide the dialogue.
