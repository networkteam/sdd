Planning the two-type redesign (d-cpt-e7i) scoped the data-layer work — migration mechanics, kind rewrites, CQRS, pre-flight updates — but did not anticipate the presentation-surface consequences of fanning out to ten kinds (5 signal + 5 decision). Executing s-tac-2bw surfaced this: the "Recent Insights" ask was small, but implementing it naturally exposed parallel questions about fact signals flooding Open Signals, activity decisions being lumped with directives, redundant "Open"/"Active" section prefixes, and ordering.

Dialogue converged on a full status-view restructure (one section per decision kind, "Gaps and Questions" naming the content, prefix cleanup) — a reshape that wasn't in any plan. It emerged only from dialoguing the execution of a smaller signal.

Meta-observation: type-system changes carry downstream presentation consequences that are easy to defer or miss at planning time. Future plans touching the type system should include an explicit "presentation surfaces" check — what changes in status, list, show, catch-up — as a plan AC or explicit scope item.
