External SDD adoption surfaced substantive summary-body drift, with three concrete failure modes observed in the same project: actor hallucination (an outcome-framed aspiration body with no actor was summarized as a self-commitment by an entity the body never named); graph-positioning commentary appended outside body scope (the summary added "builds on X, operationalizes Y" style text describing graph relationships not present in the body); and identifier-casing auto-correction that overrode intentional brand styling. Because `sdd status`, lists, and catch-up render the summary exclusively, drift creates a visibility asymmetry — the body remains the immutable source of truth, but readers and agents consume the divergent visible version, sometimes for days. Stored summaries are necessary for efficiency at scale: status, lists, and catch-up render compressed text across hundreds of entries per invocation, ruling out per-read regeneration. None of the three repair paths cleanly resolve substantive drift once it has occurred: in-place edits break immutability and stale `summary_hash`; supersede is heavyweight when only the representation is wrong; LLM regeneration can introduce its own drift. Candidate directions for later dialogue: harden the summarizer prompt with explicit preservation rules (no-actor-invention when body has none, casing preservation, scope restriction); define the intended scope of the summary artifact (body-only versus body-plus-immediate-refs, which would legitimize graph-positioning commentary); add lint or diff tooling to detect drift between body and summary as ongoing hygiene; and provide explicit summary control via tooling — let `sdd new` accept a user-supplied summary that bypasses LLM generation at capture, and `sdd summarize` accept either explicit text or a stronger model selector to replace a drifted summary, with hash recomputed against the new text. Refines the cross-model summary template insight (s-cpt-ed2) by separating drift after generation from drift during generation, and surfaces tension with the immutability contract (d-cpt-e1i) where the spirit (no parallel records) is violated even when the letter (no body edits) holds.
