The two-type redesign (d-cpt-omm) lacks a decision kind for perpetual aspirational direction — something participants commit to working toward that has no completion gate. Proposes adding `aspiration` as a fifth decision kind alongside directive/contract/plan/task, functioning as an attractor (pull toward) distinct from contract's constraint (push against). Multiple aspirations can coexist in tension — a constellation rather than a single north star — so individual decisions are evaluated on alignment rather than compliance. Larger-scope aspirations tend to decay slower than directives: they sit further from execution, so new learning reshapes them less often than nearby commitments.

See [proposal](./19-113352-s-cpt-7we/proposal.md) for the full synthesis: story evidence, push-vs-pull distinction, constellation framing, mutability / decay rate, and naming trade-offs.
