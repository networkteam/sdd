Context reuse is already the differentiator in agentic engineering: practitioners build personal "hoards" of research notes and solved problems to feed future agents. SDD provides a structured alternative — the graph is machine-addressable context, semantically linked and layered by thinking depth, making the direction-as-bottleneck insight (s-stg-jbo) actionable. The key distinction from low-friction "process everything" tools (MemPalace et al.) is *how* context enters the graph: through dialogue rather than automated ingestion. Dialogue is the sieve — it filters signal from noise and captures what matters. This conversation is a live example: relevant insights from an external source surfaced through dialogue, not bulk import.
