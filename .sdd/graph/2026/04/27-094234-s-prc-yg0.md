First external evaluation of `/sdd-bootstrap` on a greenfield German-language project (Viebrockhaus chatbot POC). The captured graph anchored real work — the functional outcome was sound — but the user experience relied on existing SDD knowledge to navigate: teaching mode leaked framework vocabulary (canonical, aliases, write-once, process-layer, Sinek) into user-facing prose; actor captures asked multiple questions per turn instead of one at a time; pre-flight's actor/role boundary rejected natural descriptions that mixed identity and project-role content, forcing `--skip-preflight` to proceed; `/sdd`'s bootstrap offer echoed internal move names; `vocabulary-de` rendered the "perpetual pull" metaphor literally as "Sog" with negative German connotation. Partitioning rich user input into distinct entry candidates, surfacing aspiration tension as a pre-flight finding, and self-correcting after blocked captures all worked well — preserve those. The full transcript and categorized issue list (A–P) are in the [attachment](./27-094234-s-prc-yg0/evaluation.md), along with non-binding improvement directions — future sessions decide between revising the skill, running a brownfield test, or both. Orthogonal concerns (pre-flight rubric calibration, `sdd init` first-time tip, auto-summary language drift) warrant separate signals later.
