Align pre-flight validation templates with the principle that dialogue drives graph evolution — validation checks whether entries faithfully capture dialogue reasoning, not whether they conform to structural ceremony. Supersedes `d-prc-22i` by absorbing its durability scope into a broader template alignment pass.

Five changes in one pass. (1) `supersedes.tmpl`: drop the type-consistency check; replace with dialogue-driven replacement — the entry text must explain what happens to the superseded entry's concerns, but the structural shape is the author's choice. (2) `decision_refs.tmpl`: narrow ref completeness from "are related signals unreferenced?" to two concrete checks: does this entry duplicate information from existing entries it should reference instead, and does it logically build upon or advance existing entries without referencing them? Refs = logical dependency, not topical association. (3) `closing_action.tmpl`: add durability section per `d-prc-22i` — three paths to durability (prior commit, attachments on this entry, attachments on referenced upstream entry), presence-based check. (4) SKILL.md line 60: replace code-changes-only framing with two-class framing (prior commit OR `--attach`). (5) `entry_quality.tmpl`: extend "dialogue context is grounding" to cover structural departures — when entry text argues for a non-standard structural move, treat the reasoning as human-confirmed context.

Excludes `closing_decision.tmpl` (deferred to two-type system `d-cpt-omm`), `contracts.tmpl` (tension is intended), `signal_capture.tmpl` (working well), `action_closes_signals.tmpl` (no issues surfaced).

See [design](./14-101221-d-tac-h3o/design.md) for proposed template wording per item.
