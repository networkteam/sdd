# Key takeaways: "The Friction Is Your Judgment"
Talk: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_Zcw_sVF6hU
Speakers: Armin Ronacher & Cristina Poncela Cubeiro (Earendil)

## Friction as the steering mechanism

> "the friction actually in many ways is what's necessary on a physical level to steer. Like without friction, there's no steering"

> "this friction... this is really where the judgment is. This is where experience is, and you should be inserting that and start feeling it"

> "we need to find a way to feel the pain that the agent doesn't feel and you need to be woken up in a way when you should be looking at this"

## The velocity trap — autonomous loops don't self-regulate

> "because we produce a lot of output very fast, we are tricked into thinking that we're actually being more efficient, doing more work. And this is quite the opposite because now we don't have as much time to actually stop and think and design what we're doing"

> "when you're in this flow, it's very difficult for yourself to stop, and it's definitely very difficult for your agent to stop because it's running around"

> "we are the ones that need to actually have the agency to being controlled here"

> "it is so devious if you start relying on its speed where you really shouldn't"

## Where to insert friction

> "we don't think that a database migration should ever go in without a human making a judgment call on this... There's just some examples where we learned, if we miss it, we regret it."

> "system architecture, creating reliability in a system, they're not just very good at. Because we really still have to go slow."
