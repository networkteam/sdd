The order of bootstrap moves feels unnatural in practice: move 3 (actor capture) asks "who are you?" before move 4 (Golden Circle / "what are you working on?"), so users describe themselves before the project context that would frame why the question matters. A candidate restructure worth thinking through: capture only the local participant up front, run the Golden Circle next, and ask about additional participants at the end — or capture additional actors opportunistically as they surface during WHY/HOW dialogue, since founders, stakeholders, or domain experts may already be implied in those answers. The trade-off is not yet clear: Golden Circle entries currently rely on actors being established for participant fields at capture time, so a restructure either defers participant attribution until after Golden Circle or captures actors mid-flow as they come up. Complements the prior bootstrap eval insight (s-prc-yg0) by surfacing a whole-flow sequencing concern beyond the per-move usability issues already documented; questions the move ordering in the bootstrap plan (d-cpt-kv1).
